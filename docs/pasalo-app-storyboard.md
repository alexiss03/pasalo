# Pasalo App Storyboard

## Purpose

This storyboard is a narrative walkthrough of the Pasalo marketplace experience as it exists in the product today. It is meant for pitching, design reviews, onboarding, and aligning product, design, and engineering on the core user journey.

## Story Arc

The story follows three parallel actors:

- Buyer: searching for a lower-entry property with clear terms
- Seller: trying to transfer a pasalo listing safely and quickly
- Admin: keeping the marketplace trustworthy and operational

## Frame 1 - The Problem

**Scene**
- A buyer is scrolling through scattered Facebook posts and inconsistent property listings.
- The seller is struggling to answer the same questions repeatedly in private messages.

**User feeling**
- Frustrated, cautious, and unsure what is real.

**Product moment**
- Pasalo Marketplace is introduced as one focused place for verified pasalo listings in Metro Manila, Laguna, and Cavite.

**Key UI**
- Homepage hero
- Filter tabs for property type and location
- Highlight sections for top listings, newest listings, and ongoing biddings

## Frame 2 - Buyer Discovers the Marketplace

**Scene**
- The buyer lands on the home page and immediately sees structured property cards with cash out and monthly figures.

**User feeling**
- Relief, because the listings are finally comparable.

**Product moment**
- The buyer uses search and filters to narrow results by condo, city, developer, budget, and verified-only listings.

**Key UI**
- Home page filters
- Category tabs
- Listing cards with:
  - title
  - project name
  - location
  - cash out
  - monthly amortization
  - verification badge

## Frame 3 - Buyer Evaluates a Listing

**Scene**
- The buyer opens a listing detail page for a condo in Quezon City.

**User feeling**
- More confident, because the page is transparent and complete.

**Product moment**
- The buyer reviews:
  - photo carousel
  - financial breakdown
  - remaining months
  - loan availability
  - turnover date
  - seller identity and verification status

**Key UI**
- Listing detail page
- Financial breakdown section
- Property details section
- Favorites button

## Frame 4 - Seller Creates a Listing

**Scene**
- A seller logs in and starts posting a pasalo property.

**User feeling**
- Guided, but aware this is a serious transaction.

**Product moment**
- The seller fills out:
  - property information
  - financials
  - uploaded photos
  - AI document verification files
  - viewing availability
  - optional auction setup
  - seller and attorney signatures
  - seller listing agreement

**Key UI**
- Create listing page
- Searchable developer dropdown
- Signature pad
- Photo upload
- Verification upload
- Draft and publish actions

## Frame 5 - Trust Is Built Before Contact

**Scene**
- Before the listing goes live, the platform validates the seller’s documents and records lead-protection terms.

**User feeling**
- Protected on both sides.

**Product moment**
- The listing becomes safer because:
  - seller identity is checked
  - listing documents are reviewed
  - agreement terms are stored
  - commission and lead validity are recorded

**Key UI**
- Verified Pasalo badge
- Identity verification indicators
- Admin verification queue

## Frame 6 - Buyer Starts the Conversation

**Scene**
- The buyer taps `Message Seller` from the listing.

**User feeling**
- Ready to ask real questions without leaving the app.

**Product moment**
- A dedicated listing conversation opens.
- Payment-related free text is blocked to keep negotiation compliant and traceable inside the product.

**Key UI**
- Messages list
- Conversation thread
- Read/unread states
- Payment-safe messaging behavior

## Frame 7 - Buyer Requests a Viewing

**Scene**
- The buyer taps `Request Viewing` and sees available seller time slots.

**User feeling**
- The process feels more organized and serious than informal chat.

**Product moment**
- The buyer chooses:
  - available date
  - time slot
  - viewing duration
  - interval-driven scheduling
  - optional notes

**Key UI**
- Viewing request modal
- Seller-defined schedule slots
- Duration and interval details

## Frame 8 - Buyer Saves and Compares

**Scene**
- The buyer is not ready to decide yet, so they save listings and compare projects.

**User feeling**
- In control instead of rushed.

**Product moment**
- The buyer:
  - saves favorites
  - browses project pages
  - sees current listings grouped under a project
  - checks category pages like apartments

**Key UI**
- Favorites
- Projects page
- Project detail page with current listings
- Apartments landing page

## Frame 9 - Seller Manages Demand

**Scene**
- The seller receives inquiries, viewing requests, and favorites from multiple buyers.

**User feeling**
- Supported, not overwhelmed.

**Product moment**
- The seller:
  - edits viewing availability
  - cancels or reschedules viewings
  - monitors listing status
  - moves the listing through its public availability lifecycle

**Key UI**
- My Properties
- Edit listing screen
- Viewing request management

## Frame 10 - Admin Moderates the Marketplace

**Scene**
- An admin opens the admin dashboard to review activity across the platform.

**User feeling**
- In control of marketplace quality and risk.

**Product moment**
- The admin can:
  - monitor summary metrics
  - approve or reject listings
  - review verification documents
  - process role applications
  - manage users
  - manage developers
  - inspect conversations, leads, payments, and audit logs

**Key UI**
- Admin dashboard
- Moderation queues
- Users tab
- Listings tab
- Payments and audit tab

## Frame 11 - Payment Stays Inside the App

**Scene**
- Once both sides are ready, the seller issues a payment request in the conversation thread.

**User feeling**
- Safer, because the payment trail stays within the platform.

**Product moment**
- The app:
  - blocks off-platform payment negotiation in free text
  - creates payment intents inside the conversation
  - supports status refresh and cancellation
  - keeps activity traceable for both ops and admin

**Key UI**
- In-app payments in message thread
- Payment request status
- Auditability in admin

## Frame 12 - Marketplace Outcome

**Scene**
- The buyer has a clear path to purchase, the seller has managed leads in one place, and the admin has kept the listing trustworthy.

**User feeling**
- Confident that the process is legitimate and trackable.

**Product moment**
- Pasalo Marketplace becomes the operating system for a transaction that normally feels scattered and risky.

**Closing message**
- Instead of messy posts and fragmented DMs, the marketplace gives buyers, sellers, and operators one structured flow from discovery to verified deal progress.

## Short Version For Presentations

Use this 6-beat version when you need a faster pitch:

1. Buyer discovers clean, verified pasalo inventory.
2. Buyer compares real cash out and monthly terms.
3. Seller creates a listing with documents, signatures, and verification.
4. Buyer messages the seller and books a viewing in-app.
5. Admin moderates trust, listings, users, and documents.
6. Payment and deal activity stay inside the platform.
